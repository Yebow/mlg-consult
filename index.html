Think  ·  Strategic Engagement  ·  6–8 weeks

From strategic fog
to a market-ready product.

A leading European streaming platform caught between two markets. 17 stakeholders across 5 functions, months of decks, zero decisions. One quarter with MLG to fix that.

17
Stakeholders aligned across product, design, marketing, tech & research
850+
Users in the quantitative study across 3 segments
51%
Retention signal among hybrid customers exposed to the new offer
What MLG did
Ran a tightly facilitated co-creation workshop with a multi-criteria decision matrix. 17 stakeholders, one morning, a shared roadmap — what usually takes 3 to 6 months of meetings.
Built multi-modal concept artifacts — explainer videos, visual mockups, written specs — calibrated to remove ambiguity. Then tested them quantitatively with 850+ representative users across DTV loyalists, potential cord-cutters, and streaming-only customers.
Delivered a feature-by-feature read on clarity, perceived value, and adoption signal — granular enough to feed straight into roadmap and pricing decisions. Also surfaced two features below comprehension threshold, early enough to rework before launch.
Output
Multi-quarter roadmap prioritized on real adoption signal. Pricing structure positioned against measured willingness to pay. Leadership aligned without political battles. Cross-functional teams executing instead of arguing.

Think  ·  Focused Diagnostic  ·  2–4 weeks

We stopped a six-figure build
the market didn't want.

A consumer platform had greenlit an AI feature with full internal buy-in — executive sponsor, engineering staffed, scope expanding. No user evidence. The cost of being wrong was growing every week.

€100k+
Engineering effort avoided and reallocated to real user problems
12%
Concept appeal — below every viability benchmark
470
Users surveyed. Results in one month.
What MLG did
Scoped the research as a decision tool, not a report. One output: a clear go/no-go signal both CPO and CFO could act on. No 60-page deck. Fifteen questions, seven minutes, representative sample, scoring framework neither side could dismiss as subjective.
Forced internal vision alignment before a single user saw the concept. Everyone signed off on what was being tested — so results couldn't be argued away as testing the wrong thing.
Corrected raw usage intent for curiosity-driven responses. 23% raw collapsed to 6% real. That's the difference between shipping a feature nobody needs and reallocating engineering to problems that actually matter.
Output
Feature stopped before a single production line was written. Six figures reallocated to problems surfaced by the same study as genuinely under-served. The company exited with a repeatable decision framework for future concepts — not a one-off study, a method.
